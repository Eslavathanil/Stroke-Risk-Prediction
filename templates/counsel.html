Psychological Care After Stroke

Mental health support plays a crucial role in transforming stroke recovery. After a stroke, many survivors face not only physical challenges but also significant psychological effects, including depression, anxiety, and cognitive impairments

Early psychological therapy, mindfulness practices, and positive psychology interventions can help break the cycle of post-stroke depression and set stroke survivors on a path toward faster and stronger healing.

Psychological support creates a virtuous cycle: improved mood and confidence encourage more effort in rehabilitation, leading to better physical recovery, which in turn further improves mental health.Early and continuous mental health care can prevent long-term psychiatric disorders and improve quality of life for stroke survivors.
